Elliot Lake

Uranium Mines


Historical Context

The Uranium Mines of Elliot Lake were opened in the early 1950's in a post-war effort to continue to supply the United States of America with uranium for nuclear weapons development. This lasted for a few years before the United States declared it would no longer purchase uranium from Canada after 1962.




Consequences to Elliot Lake

The waste produced from the Uranium Mines was dumped into ten of the surrounding lakes, killing them all, as well as 90km of the Serpent River. The radioactive waste was not only killing the lakes and rivers, but was also effecting the people, animals, and environment in the Elliot Lake region, as they all became exposed to increased levels of radiation.


Wartime Nuclear Involvement

Although the Uranium Mines in Elliot Lake were opened after the end of World War II, the mines supplied the United States with uranium to continue their nuclear weapons research. The majority of the world's uranium came from the Elliot Lake Uranium Mines, from Cold War nuclear arms, to nuclear energy research.
